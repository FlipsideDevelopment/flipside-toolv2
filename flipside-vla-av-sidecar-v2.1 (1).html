Annotator Workspace
📋 Task Overview & Guidelines
▾
Task 1 — Captioning (Facts + Reasoning → Caption) You are reviewing a ~8-second driving clip. For each clip you are shown model-generated facts and reasoning. Your job is to review them and provide corrections only where something is incorrect, misleading, missing an important detail, or incorrectly explains ego decision-making. You are not writing the final caption — your corrections are synthesized automatically.
Task 2 — Action Justification Review or provide the ego vehicle action (e.g., slow down, stop, yield, proceed, change lane) and write a short natural-language justification describing why that action is taken. Action–justification pairs are used for downstream language–action grounding training.
Note on ❌ / ✅ markers
The ❌ and ✅ symbols appear only in training samples and annotator references — they are not present in real model output. The actual Original Facts and Original Reasoning from the annotation platform are plain numbered lists. You determine what needs correction by watching the clip.
When to correct
✏
Incorrect — the fact or reasoning is factually wrong about the scene.
⚠
Misleading — the statement is technically true but creates a wrong impression.
➕
Missing detail — an important driving-relevant or safety-relevant detail is omitted.
🔄
Wrong ego reasoning — incorrect explanation of right-of-way, yielding, or hazard awareness.
What to focus on
🚗
Ego motion and intent — speed, slowing, stopping, yielding, lane position
👥
Other road users — vehicles, pedestrians, cyclists and their relevance to ego decisions
🚦
Traffic controls — signals, signs, road markings and their driving implications
⚡
Hazards and right-of-way — obstructions, conflicts, yielding interactions
Important rules
✅
Only correct what is wrong. If facts and reasoning are accurate, accept them as-is — do not rewrite for style.
🚫
Do not invent scene details. Only describe objects and events visibly present in the clip.
📏
Speed values. Use ego speed only if explicitly provided in the metadata. Otherwise use relative phrasing: "moving slowly", "decelerating to stop" — never invent mph/km/h.
⏱
Timestamps are optional. Facts/reasoning describe the clip overall. Add approximate times (e.g., ~4s) only if a mid-clip change is driving-relevant.

ℹ Fact Review Guidelines
▾
Model-Generated Facts describe the driving scene. Review each fact and accept it if correct, or edit it if it is incorrect, misleading, or missing a safety-relevant detail.
✅
Accept facts that correctly describe observable scene elements: road layout, other road users, traffic controls, ego position, or relevant hazards. Most facts will be correct — only edit what is genuinely wrong.
✎
Edit facts that are incorrect, hallucinated, misleading, or missing a safety-relevant detail. Write your correction in any language — it will be normalized into English.
📏

↔

🚫

👁
Only describe what is visible. Do not speculate about intent, causes, or off-camera events. ✗ A vehicle appears to be merging into the lane. ✓ A vehicle is merging from the right lane ahead.
⏱
Timestamps are optional. Facts describe the clip overall (~8 seconds). Add an approximate time (e.g., ~4s) only if a mid-clip change is driving-relevant.

Task 1b — Reasoning Review
ℹ Reasoning Review Guidelines
▾
Model-Generated Reasoning explains why the ego vehicle behaved as it did. Correct it if the reasoning is wrong, vague, generic, or incorrectly explains ego decision-making (right-of-way, yielding, hazard awareness).
🔗
Required structure: visible cue → ego vehicle response. Both parts must be present and grounded. ✗ The ego vehicle drives carefully near the junction. ✓ A pedestrian is crossing at the junction ahead, so the ego vehicle slows down and prepares to stop.
📏
Wrong speed reference → replace with relative description. ✗ My slow speed of 4 mph is appropriate for the sharp turn. [exact invented speed] ✓ My moderate speed is appropriate for the wet conditions and curvature of the turn.
↔
Wrong direction in reasoning → must match what is visible. Reasoning errors often mirror fact errors — double-check left/right references. ✗ I need to monitor the pedestrian on the left to ensure they do not step into the road. ✓ I need to remain aware of the pedestrian on the right to confirm they stay on the sidewalk.
🔮
Speculation → replace with observable present state. Do not predict future events — describe what is currently happening. ✗ The bus may stop to let passengers on or off, potentially causing them to cross the road. ✓ The red double-decker bus ahead does not currently obstruct my path but remains within observation.
🚫
Generic phrases are never acceptable: ✗ "drive carefully" / "be cautious" / "stay alert" / "drive safely" Replace with a grounded cue-response statement referencing what is actually visible.
👁
The cue must be visually observable. Reject reasoning that references sensors, AI systems, or events not visible in the clip. ✗ The system detects a hazard and applies automatic braking. ✓ A vehicle ahead brakes suddenly, so the ego vehicle decelerates to maintain a safe gap.
🔄
Correct wrong ego decision-making explanations. If the model incorrectly explains right-of-way, yielding priority, or hazard awareness, edit it to reflect what actually happened in the clip.
💬 Write in any language — Tagalog, Bisaya, Taglish, or English

Task 2 — Action Justification
ℹ Action Justification Guidelines
▾
Provide the ego vehicle's action and a short natural-language justification explaining why that action was taken. These pairs are used for language–action grounding in downstream model training.
🎯
Required structure: [visible cue], so the ego vehicle [action]. Both the cue and the action must be explicit. ✓ A pedestrian is crossing ahead, so the ego vehicle slows down and prepares to stop. ✓ The traffic light ahead turns red, so the ego vehicle comes to a full stop at the line. ✓ A vehicle is merging from the right, so the ego vehicle yields and reduces speed.
📍
The critical cue must name the specific visible trigger. Be precise — name the road user, object, or traffic control that directly caused the action. ✗ unsafe road conditions ✓ a pedestrian stepping off the curb ahead
🚫
Never use vague justification language. ✗ The ego vehicle slows down to be safe. ✗ The driver decides to slow down carefully. ✗ The ego vehicle proceeds with caution.
⚡
Typical cue → action pairings: slow down → vehicle/pedestrian/cyclist ahead, speed change ahead, sharp curve stop → red traffic light, stop sign, pedestrian in crosswalk, blocked junction yield → oncoming traffic, merging vehicle, right-of-way conflict, junction priority maintain reduced speed → narrow lane, obscured sightlines, following a slow vehicle proceed cautiously → construction zone, wet road, limited visibility change lane → obstruction ahead, slower vehicle, lane ends
📏
Speed in justifications. Same rule as facts — use relative descriptions unless metadata provides exact values. ✗ The ego vehicle decelerates to 15 km/h. ✓ The ego vehicle decelerates significantly and prepares to stop.
